# ⭐ **THE 5‑MINUTE TRADER —  STRATEGY DOCUMENT**

## 🔰 **UPFRONT RULES (Read Before Trading)**

### **Account & Position Sizing**
- Trade **20% of your account only**  
- You can split this 20% across **multiple contracts**  
- **Avoid contracts under $100** (too volatile, too fast)

### **Expiration**
- Use **0DTE** (zero days to expiration)  
- Or **tomorrow’s expiration**  
- Usually trade **SPX**, sometimes **SPY**

### **What the Strategy Is**
The 5‑minute trading strategy uses the **first 5‑minute candle** to aim for a **10–20 point price target on ES**.

We **chart ES**  
We **trade SPX options**  
**ES is the msot accurate represenation of market direction, so we sue it to chart price lines before trading SPX options. Both ES and SPX are the S&P 500. SPX moces with ES, but pys more for the same market move. Thnk of ES as he map and SPX as he car you use to drive.**
**10 points on ES is ALWAYS the first target.**

The strategy uses:
- **9 / 21 / 200 EMAs** for confluence  
- **1‑hour chart** to determine trend  
- **Two trade opportunities**  
  - **Trade 1** (first 5‑minute candle scalp)  
  - **Trade 2** (retest & continuation)  
- Some days you **skip Trade 1** if the market is too choppy.

# ⭐ **THE TWO 5‑MINUTE STRATEGIES**
There are **two** ways to trade the 5‑minute strategy:

1. **Strategy 1 — First 5‑Minute Candle Break (Scalp)**  
2. **Strategy 2 — Retest & Continuation (Safer)**  

Some days you **only use Strategy 2** if the market is too choppy.

---

# ⭐ **5‑MINUTE STRATEGY 1 — FIRST 5‑MINUTE CANDLE BREAK (SCALP)**  
**We chart ES, but we trade SPX options.**

## **Pre‑Market Preparation on ES**
1. Mark **pre‑market resistance and support lines**  
2. Mark **9/21 EMA cross** and **200 EMA**  
   - On the **1‑day chart**  
   - On the **4‑hour chart**

3. Move to the **5‑minute chart**  
   - **Look left** for pre‑market choppiness  
   - If choppy → **draw a chop box**  
     - Choppy = many inside candles, sideways movement  
   - Mark any **pre‑market price levels** on the 5‑minute chart  
     - These become **targets** and **exit zones**

---

## **Mark the First 5‑Minute Candle**
4. Mark the **top** and **bottom** of the first 5‑minute candle  
5. Draw a line **10 points above** the top  
6. Draw a line **10 points below** the bottom  
   - These lines mark where you would exit the trade

7. Identify any **support or resistance lines** in the breakout direction  
   - These may reject price  
   - These may become your exit target

---

## **Entry Logic**
8. If pre‑market was **choppy**, do NOT enter immediately  
   Wait for the first candle to:

   - Break out of the chop box  
   - AND break one of the 5‑minute lines  
   - Even if the candle is half inside / half outside the box

9. When this breakout happens → **enter the trade**

---

## **Exit Logic**
10. Exit when price reaches the **nearest support or resistance line**  
11. Ideally this exit should be **at least 10 points** from the nearest 5‑minute line  
    - This satisfies your **10‑point target**

---

# ⭐ **5‑MINUTE STRATEGY 2 — RETEST & CONTINUATION (SAFER)**  
Used when the market has a **chop box** or when Strategy 1 is too risky.

## **Entry Logic**
1. Wait for a candle to break out of the chop box **completely**  
2. The breakout candle must **float** above or below the box  
3. Breakout must match the **9/21 EMA cross direction**  
   - Bullish cross → breakout bar must be **green**  
   - Bearish cross → breakout bar must be **red**

4. Enter once the breakout candle confirms direction

---

## **Exit Logic**
5. Exit when price reaches the **next resistance or support line**  
6. Confirm momentum using the **9 EMA**  
   - Rising trend → candles track **above** the 9 EMA  
   - Falling trend → candles track **below** the 9 EMA

7. Your exit target is **20 points** from entry

---

## **Trailing Stop / Runner Logic**
8. Set two planned exits:
   - **10‑point exit**  
   - **20‑point exit**

9. At **10 points**, sell most of your contracts  
10. Keep **1 or a few runners**  
11. Runners exit only when the **20‑point target** is hit

---

# ⭐ **TRADE 1 — FIRST 5‑MINUTE CANDLE BREAK (SCALP)**

### **Pre‑Market Prep (on ES)**
1. Mark pre‑market **support/resistance**.  
2. Mark **9/21 EMA cross** + **200 EMA** on **1D** and **4H**.  
3. On the **5‑minute chart**:  
   - Look left for **choppiness** → draw **chop box** if needed.  
   - Mark pre‑market levels (targets/exits).

### **Mark the First Candle**
4. Mark **top** and **bottom** of the first 5‑minute candle.  
5. Draw a line **10 points above** the top.  
6. Draw a line **10 points below** the bottom.  
7. Identify any **support/resistance** in breakout direction.

### **Entry**
8. If choppy → wait for candle to:  
   - Break out of chop box  
   - AND break one of the 10‑point lines  
   - Even if half‑in/half‑out  
9. Enter on breakout.

### **Exit**
10. Exit at nearest **support/resistance**.  
11. Ideally ≥ **10 points** from breakout line.

---

# ⭐ **TRADE 2 — RETEST & CONTINUATION (SAFER)**

### **Entry**
1. Wait for candle to break **fully** out of chop box.  
2. Candle must **float** above/below the box.  
3. Breakout must match **9/21 EMA cross direction**  
   - Bullish → green bar  
   - Bearish → red bar  
4. Enter on confirmed breakout.

### **Exit**
5. Exit at next **support/resistance**.  
6. Confirm momentum with **9 EMA**:  
   - Uptrend → candles above 9 EMA  
   - Downtrend → candles below 9 EMA  
7. Target = **20 points**.

### **Trailing Stop / Runners**
8. Two exits: **10 points** and **20 points**.  
9. Sell most at **10 points**.  
10. Keep **runners** until **20 points**.
